Topic

The team at IDK-5 plan to be at the forefront of crisis management. Our current project is designed to help the general public to understand their position as consumers during national turmoil. Whilst the pandemic of COVID-19 has bought the need for supermarket stock control to the forefront, it is far from the first disaster Australia has had which impacted logistics or product availability. Our teams long term goal is to prevent misinformation and create a clear guide on the current enforced purchasing regulations during each individual crisis. In addition to this, the project will also provide consumers with information of current seasonable product, product defect notices and product unavailability.

Motivation

Everyone in the team from IDK-5 team comes from different backgrounds, yet all were impacted by the panic buying associated with the current pandemic. It became difficult to source certain products, no matter which state you lived in. The restrictions also became almost impossible to follow as there was no clear overall rule regarding purchases. Realistically this project fits in with the only IT trend that remains constant: convenience. It is designed to make things simple and straightforward. This will save time for the busy father on the go, help the elderly grandmother to know exactly what the rules are and the single student to avoid spending their studying hours wandering from store to store looking for toilet paper. If showed to an employer, this project would identify the team’s ability to focus on a broader picture rather than simply the effects on the community in the moment. By identifying a problem which affects such a large consumer base, creating a resolution and liaising with multiple government branches; the team has identified that it is more than capable of handling the most complex of tasks.

Landscape

This project is based on a smaller scale technology utilised largely in restaurants to track inventory and sales, an example of this can be found in applications Kounta, Lavu and JumpStock. The difference is that this product will be government supported, cover a variety of large supermarket chains, keep customer’s up to date with purchasing restrictions and be functional nationwide.
