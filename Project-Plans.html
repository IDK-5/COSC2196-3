Plans and Progress

After A2 as a team we had decided to change lanes on our project idea. Ultimately, we concluded that a project to develop a sophisticated nation-wide disaster relief resource management and distribution tool for government bodies to utilise was the path we would like to explore. Coronavirus brought to our attention the effects panic buying can have not only on supermarkets, but supply chains nationally, the elderly and overall the general consumer. Generating a conversation on how not only in times such as pandemics but this project could also be off high value during Australia’s notorious natural disaster seasons. Australia is ranked ninth in the world for natural disasters costing Australia $37 Billion in the years of 2008 – 2018. (Letts S 2018, parra. 1) Human emotion during these times leads many individuals and families raiding supermarket shelfs for anything they can get their hands on to stock up out of fear. This Psychology has been ever present through disasters, pandemics, wars and so forth as far back as WW1 (Wikipedia, n.a) There is also a very clear correlation between the functional disturbance of business caused by natural disasters impacting surrounding areas quality of life. (Fujita M 2017, Ian Burke M & Wisetjindawata W, 2017, parra .1) The team decided to call our project ComCare.

Overall there are two main areas of resource management we would like to improve during these times. The first of which is prioritised delivery of essential understocked goods into disaster struck immediate and surrounding areas. The first step of this process is determining the needs for people in these areas. We aim to develop a web interface and application where citizens can register their location and any specialised needs they have. This will generate a logistical framework for the demand in the area giving the system an outline to determine the essential requirements of all effected, minimising the overall impacts and aiding with their recovery.

The next step in building a fundamentally sound understanding off the potential resource distribution available is to build of a communication layer from the major supermarkets in the area. Here we aim to capture inventory levels for essential goods in surrounding supermarkets. With both the user requirements and current stock on hand from supermarkets data this will generate an understating off what items will be in shortfall for these areas.

The objective to capturing this data and determining the supply and demand for essential goods in the affected areas is to provide respective government bodies the metrics to intervene when necessary. The foremost objective would be for these bodies to take this information and work with logistical chains to help increase supply as priority for the currently understocked essential goods to the areas desperately in need of these products. Through this collaboration we hope to see a highly targeted plan and approach of action based on ComCares data to help provide for affected areas. Mitigation of the issue whilst always is the team’s number one priority we understand that this may not always be possible. The secondary area of functionality our project aims to deliver is the ability for consumer purchasing to be limited based on supply to deteriorate the effects of panic buying.

The secondary functionality like the first would consist of multiple elements. Taking the supply and demand data, if there is a determination that indeed there is a significant undersupply of essential items and any kind of panic buying will severely impact the lives of others, we aim to build in a way for the purchasing off essential goods to be limited. This will ensure the goods are evenly distributed across the community and no one is left without especially the most vulnerable.

The first step to this process is through the for mentioned web and application user interface a limitation per family is to be communicated. This will ensure that all individuals in the area have an understanding off their purchasing limitations based on the current availability of goods. The next step of the process is to build an interface at the checkout in a supermarket. The interface will allow the user to tap a card of some kind (Potentially using NFC through the phone application) communicating to the checkout system the individual’s current status on any items with limitations. Then building in functionality to reject purchases if they are over the allocation. The final step of the process is once the transaction is completed then to relay the information back to ComCares infrastructure, recalculating the users remaining purchases available.

This process will give the system a way to elevate any kind of panic buying and build a real representation of user demand in these areas to then improve the prioritisation off inbound inventory. The ability to enforce said limitations ensures that all member of the public effected by disasters do not have the compounding pressure of competing with their neighbours to get the essential items they need. Furth more this also takes the unnecessary pressure of the supply chain, warehouses, retail staff and supermarkets from consumers overbuying goods they could not possible ever use.

Overall the objective for the project is to build a well-rounded multi-dimensional tool for resource management in disaster struck areas. Providing firstly valuable analytics for mitigation to help improve supply for the areas most effected. Secondly providing the capacity if needed to step in and ensure the fair distribution of essential items to all in need elevating the cascading effects of panic buying both on the logistical systems and consumers quality of life.

For the project to be successful we will need to establish a robust entity relationship throughout the system. Below we have pictured the current standing on this relationship. There were many considerations to be made here.

Figure 4. ERD for the ComCare Database Management System

Firstly, that of the structure and definition of a Family, we had decided to cater for many scenarios in the structure allowing for one family to be spread across multiple addresses and multiple families to be living at the same address. This will take into consideration situations where individuals complete the shopping for others not at home, for example elderly parents and situations of shared accommodation where many families are living at the same address. The only limitation that will be enforced is that each individual can only be a part of one family structure. We will also be asking for a primary address to be populated in the family table, this will be simple to send any mail to if necessary.

Also implement the capacity to assign a temporary nominee, this will be extremely usefully for age and disability care facilities. Whereby there may be a different individual who is doing the shopping for a user. In situations where someone is shopping on behalf of another person allowing the user to nominate another member of the public for a period of time will ensure no one is left out even if this person is not within their family structure.

We have currently also designed the progressive home user interface for logging into the ComCare application where users can register their details, validate a family structure, assign a temporary nominee and track any limitations for essential goods in there surrounding areas.

Figure 5. Possible design concepts.

Another consideration is the double handling off data, as every barcode is unique using the UPC system we will use this information as the Product_ID. (Range Me 2020) We could have created our own unique item numbers for all essential goods however this would be unnecessary as this system is already in place. Another example of this is the store ID, however in this case we need to take the opposite stance, we could use the ABN of the store however if the store owner owned multiple stores under the same ABN this would cause issues, thus we will need to assign unique ID to each store within the system.

Another discussion that took place was in respect to the checkout interface. Firstly, we needed to decide how the user would ID themselves to the system at a supermarket checkout. We concluded at a multi solution response, the user can either use NFC on the mobile device or they will be sent an NFC card as an alternative. As a backup, if the user loses their card and doesn’t have a phone with NFC capabilities, the user will be able to login to the ComCare website and print off a temporary QR code that can be scanned whilst their replacement card arrives.

Figure 6. Design concepts of the Government issued ComCare card.

Secondly there was a discussion about how the terminal would engage with the ComCare system at the checkout. The first option would be that every time an item is scanned it would send a request to the system checking if the item was restricted and the family’s current remaining purchases. However, we opted against this path instead the checkout will download the table of all current restricted essential goods and the families remaining allocation. As the items are scanned the table will be updated, blocking any transactions over the limitation, then sending the information back to the centralised databases once the transaction is complete. This will add a marginal increase in compute required at the local checkout terminal however will remove the huge amount of network traffic required with the alternative.

Figure 7. Application flow chart.

Another consideration would be in the situation where two individuals from the same family scan their cards at once, we need to deal with this as this could be an easy way for users to take advantage of the system. To mitigate this issue there is an activity indication in the table, if the table is active in another cahier system both cashier systems will update their tables and any other active tables. Ensuring that collectively the limitation isn’t exceeded.

At the checkout we intend to include an indicator system for the cashier, if there are no current restrictions on the store, which would be majority of the time, no scanning will be required of the pass. Alternatively, if there are current restrictions tags must be scanned before processing. This will again preserve network bandwidth and in majority of cases allow the checkout process to be undisturbed.

Finally, ComCare also aim to have local backups of family ID and current purchases against restricted essential items stored locally at the supermarkets. Natural disasters can often leave effected areas communication systems down for quite some time. To ensure the safety of the system during these times this will ensure that there is no downtime whilst communications are down. The supermarket interface will switch to the local infrastructure during this time.

Overall the group was limited due to our lack of programming experience however, we were able to construct a fundamentally sound skeleton model working through problems and feature sets of the project. producing a strong project and action plan to move forward assuming that we were able to secure funding this will allow the project to work with a development team and fulfil the desired goals of the project.

Video 1. ComCare video presentation.

The next major step will be to build off the skeleton model to finalise the proof of concept deliverable and present this information to the relevant government bodies to secure approval and funding for ComCare. Without support from said bodies much of this project will unfortunately be unrealistic to implement. The project will need their support and collaboration to help with both the implementation of mitigation and the enforcement of restrictions on low inventory count essential goods. Another element we will need to secure is sufficient funding to ensure the project is financially sustainable to bring us to full national deployment. This will allow the team to onboard programming and infrastructure developments to aid with the technical and logistical elements of the project.

Once funding is secured, we would be able to progress through the development and testing stage with the onset of full delivery and national roll out. Throughout this process we will need to work through public testing to ensure the reliability and functionality of ComCare. Overall for this process we aim to have 5,000 public users test from all parts of Australia to ensure a diverse and widespread testing environment. The way we aim to secure these numbers is through collaboration with supermarkets funding a campaign where users who participate will received $20 of their next supermarket purchases. Aiming here to secure an agreement with the supermarkets for financial collaboration keeping the public testing below $100,000.
