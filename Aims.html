Whats the Aim?

The overall aim for ComCare is to develop a sophisticated nation-wide disaster relief resource management and distribution tool for government bodies to utilise. Australia is no stranger to natural disasters, victim to fires, doubts. Floods, cyclones and much more. Australia is ranked ninth in the world for natural disasters costing Australia $37 Billion in the years of 2008 – 2018. (Letts S 2018, parra. 1)

Often during these times, it leaves surrounding neighbourhoods struggling to get resources they desperately need due to two main factors. Firstly, the effect on logistical chains, whilst households in the directly affected areas will most probably be evacuated the surrounding neighbourhoods supply chains are detrimentally interrupted during these times due to the disaster at hand. Comcare aims to deliver a tool helping elevate this problem. Collaborating with major supermarket outlets to determine their current inventory levels of essential items and what the needs of the people in the area are. Utilising this information to help navigate supply chains ensuring these areas are top priority for the supplies they need.

The second major element effecting critical supplies in these areas is the unfortunate implications of panic buying by some consumers. The direct impact off panic buying is some consumers hoarding essential items leaving nothing for others. This behaviour also has devastating effects on supply chains and charities such as foodbank. (Ky J & Peek K 2020, parra. 2) Supermarket chains like Coles, Woolworths, Aldi and so forth where forced to put limits on essential item purchasing to help mitigate this behaviour during the international COVID19 pandemic. (The Guardian 2020) Comcares secondary objective is when needed to aid in the fair distribution off goods in disaster struck areas to ensure all families can obtain the goods they require.

Overall the aim is to develop a tool which will help disaster struck areas manage their resources when the time comes. With the priority being on ensuring essential supply to the areas in need, mitigating the effects on the families who have found themselves in this situation. Building a multi-dimensional tool to firstly increase the priority and navigate supply chain limitations to ensure the supermarkets have access to the stock they need. Then if needed having the ability to limit the distribution of goods in the areas diminishing the effects of panic buying.

Whilst the software and data management development are extremely import to ComCare, it is also imperative goal to ensure we have both government and supermarket support for ComCare. For the system to work in the way we intend. Without their support we wouldn’t have the capacity to influence such management of logistics and consumer purchases. Secondly funding would become a prevalent issue long term to sustain the development of the idea. The group will aim to develop a presentable deliverable for review to relevant government bodies early in the project plan. This will enable the security of both support and funding for the project

The secondary objective will be the development of the data management and retail store interface to mitigation low inventory levels for supermarkets in disaster struck areas. To achieve this, we will be required to build an inventory data management system where stock levels off essential items are communicated to a centralised database nationally from all major supermarkets. This will give the overarching bodies capacity when there is a disaster to determine what is needed then working with logistical and retail partners priorities delivery of goods to these areas.

The final major objective will be the consumer experience interface. Unfortunately, in some instances we understand mitigation strategies can only go so far requiring further intervention. To ensure we reduce the effects of panic buying and allow essential items to be evenly distributed across all in the affected area there will need to be a system to limit the amount an individual and family can purchase based on their needs. This will require both a way for the consumer to know what is limited and them allocate.
