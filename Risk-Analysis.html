Financial

There are two major business risks when navigating our project, both stemming from government and organizational support. whilst our project aims to provide logistical disaster relief and resource management capabilities to the nation for this to ever materialize both government bodies and major organizations need to come to the party and collaborate.

As there will need to be intervention on logistical chains and consumer consumption this indeed necessitates government body involvement. This may very well be the biggest hurdle for our project and without would be almost impossible to implement. Therefore, as part of our risk mitigation strategy we will be looking to secure approval and funding from government once we have a working deliverable to present. Once approval has been granted the next process will be to seek collaboration backed by government entities from major suppliers such as Woolworths, Coles, Aldi and the likes. It is imperative the mission of the project is clearly understood and united on between all stakeholders to ensure the project can be implemented successfully. Overall the above will be the biggest business challenge and risk for the project, without the successful collaboration there is the potential for the project to never progress thus it will be at the forefront for focus to managing the projects risk.

The next major business risk is the necessity to secure funding. Due to the nature and scale of the project funding from external parties such as government bodies will be critical to ensure its successful implementation. Development of a clear, strategical and realistic financial plan will be necessary to secure funding from said entities. Unfortunately, it is impossible to predict the exact cost and timeline for such a project however, within reason we must ensure the financial plan is as accurate and justifiable as possible whilst also allowing for unforeseen cost to both finance and time. The financial plan will be imperative to secure funding and ensure the project does come to life.

The team at IDK-5 acknowledges that the greatest risk to their company aside from financial is the security of their computers. As a result, the team has put together a multi-tiered approach to ensure it limits any sort of outsider intrusion or data loss.

Computer Security

All computers will be designed around the use of a RAID 6. This will ensure that every hard drive has a balance of fault tolerance and speed. This will prevent any lost data should there be an unexpected shut down or hard drive failure. In addition to this, each computer will have Norton Anti-Virus and its associated plug ins installed to fend off external forces. This plan in conjunction with the use of Citrix to create a virtual server with change tracking will help to hold everyone accountable for the use of company information, ensure a frequent changing of passwords, two step authentication procedures and allow oversight for team leaders.

Cloud Function

Given current global debate regarding the freedoms of E2E security and cloud use, the team at IDK-5 decided that it would be best to utilise another company right here on Australian soil. Having researched prior to our selection, Datcom came out on top. In addition to having two decades of experience, they also specialise in Google integration and Citrix. This will assist with further security control and program implementation.

Oversight

Whilst trust is important in every employee and employer relationship, there will be a strong and structured review schedule of all employee work and feedback to help identify any blockers in progress or potential holes in our security network.
